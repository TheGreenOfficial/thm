**Learn about crawlers**  <br>
**This section explains the use of crawlers and the “How” a website is indexed. A web crawler is an automated bot that discovers websites, follows links, and indexes their content (like keywords) so search engines can quickly return relevant results.**

**Search Engine Optimisation**  <br>
**SEO (Search Engine Optimization) is the process of improving a website’s structure, content, and accessibility so search engines like Google rank it higher in results based on factors like performance, crawlability, and relevance.**

**robots.txt**  <br>
**robots.txt is a simple text file at the root of a site that tells search engine crawlers which pages or folders they’re allowed or not allowed to index, but it doesn’t actually protect anything from access**

<br>

**Sitemaps**  <br>
**sitemap is a structured file that lists all important pages on a website so search engine crawlers can easily find and index content without having to guess or explore blindly.**

**What is google dorking?**  <br>
**google dorking is using advanced search operators in Google to find specific or hidden information (like files, pages, or directories) that are publicly indexed but not easily discoverable through normal searches**
